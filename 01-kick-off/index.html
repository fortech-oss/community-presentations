Why Python?

In most languages, switching the values of
variables it will look like this:


tmp = a
a = b
b = tmp

and in Python:


a, b = b, a
You will find yourself pleasantly surprised to see how easy it is to concentrate on the solution to the problem rather than the syntax and structure of the language you are programming in. When you write programs in Python, you never need to bother about the low-level details such as managing the memory, file handles, sockets, etc. Guido van Rossum, the creator of the Python language, named the language after the BBC show "Monty Pythonâ€™s Flying Circus".

Governing principles

Liberal contribution
the people who do most work are recognized as most influential

and
Benevolent dictatorship
one person (project owner) has the final say on all major decisions

Open: We all have access to the information and materials necessary for doing our best work.

Collaboration: Pair programming, feedback, demo.

Iterate: Rapid prototypes can lead to rapid discoveries.

Community: Good ideas can come from anywhere and the best ideas should win. Shared values guide decision making and community goals supersede individuals.
